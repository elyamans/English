How to Write a Narrative

A narrative tells a story — real or imagined — with a clear beginning, middle, and end.

  • 1. Orientation: Introduce the setting, time, and main characters.
  • 2. Problem/Rising Action: Build tension or a challenge that the character faces.
  • 3. Climax: The most intense or emotional point of the story.
  • 4. Resolution: Show how the problem is solved or how the character changes.
  • 5. Language: Use vivid verbs, sensory details, and emotions to engage readers.

How to Write a Descriptive Text

Descriptive writing helps readers visualize scenes by using sensory and figurative language.

Tip: Remember “SHOW, DON’T TELL.” Use sight, sound, smell, touch, and taste to bring your writing to life.

  • Wide Angle Shot (Introduction): Set the scene clearly.
  • Zoom In (Body): Focus on specific details or objects with vivid description.
  • Wide Angle Shot (Ending): Pull back, reflect, and end with closure or mood.
